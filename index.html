Wildlife of srilanka


Nestled in the heart of the Indian Ocean, Sri Lanka stands as a captivating testament to the wonders of biodiversity. This island nation's landscape is a tapestry of diverse ecosystems, ranging from the enchanting depths of lush rainforests to the sun-kissed expanses of dry plains and the rhythmic ebb and flow of coastal areas. It is within this mosaic of environments that Sri Lanka's rich wildlife flourishes, offering a mesmerizing blend of endemic species found nowhere else on Earth, alongside those shared with its neighboring countries. The charismatic Sri Lankan leopard prowls through the dense foliage, while the vibrant purple-faced langur swings gracefully from treetop to treetop. The gentle giants, Sri Lankan elephants, command attention in both the wild and protected reserves. The symphony of avian life echoes in over 430 bird species, some migratory, creating a melody that resonates through national parks like Yala and Sinharaja. As Sri Lanka navigates the delicate balance between conservation and development, its commitment to safeguarding this natural heritage reflects a recognition of the intrinsic value of its wildlife, ensuring that future generations can continue to marvel at the splendor of the island's diverse and captivating fauna.


Kumana National Park spreads over an area of 35,664 hectares. In the west, the park is bordered by River Kumubukkan Oya; to the south is south-eastern coast that runs to Panama. A 200 hectares mangrove swamp called “Kumana Villu” within the Park is subject to occasional inundation with sea water.
